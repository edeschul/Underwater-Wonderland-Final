Epipelagic Zone
Zone 1
It is the top layer of the ocean, where sunlight reaches and supports a diverse array of marine life. The epipelagic zone is typically 50–200 meters deep. It is the only oceanic layer where enough sunlight penetrates to support photosynthesis, making it a thriving habitat for phytoplankton, seaweed, and diverse marine life such as fish, sea turtles, jellyfish, and dolphins. The warm temperatures and high oxygen levels in this zone create an ideal environment for many species, including those that form coral reefs. As the most explored and utilized part of the ocean, the sunlight zone plays a crucial role in fishing, tourism, and transportation, while also serving as the foundation of the marine food chain.
