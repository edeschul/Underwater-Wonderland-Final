Mesopelagic Zone
Zone 2
Sunlight is extremely dim here, making it impossible for photosynthesis to occur. As a result, marine life in this zone has adapted to low-light conditions, with many species developing large eyes, bioluminescence, and specialized hunting techniques. Creatures such as lanternfish, squid, bristlemouths, and jellyfish thrive in this shadowy world, while some animals migrate upward at night to feed in what is known as the diel vertical migration, the largest daily movement of biomass on Earth. The water pressure increases significantly with depth, and oxygen levels begin to decrease, making survival challenging. Despite its mysterious nature, the twilight zone plays a crucial role in the carbon cycle, as marine snow—organic material from the surface—drifts down and provides nourishment for deep-sea organisms.
